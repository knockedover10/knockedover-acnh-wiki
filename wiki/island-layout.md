# 🗾 Island Layout

[← Back to README](../README.md)

---

## 🌿 Current Island Concept

A fully traditional Japanese island. The aesthetic draws from Edo-period villages, Shinto shrines, bamboo forests, and natural onsen towns. No modern elements. Every structure, path, and plant choice should reinforce this theme.

---

## 🗺️ Long-Term Layout Vision

The island is divided into distinct districts, each with a clear purpose and atmosphere. Movement between districts should feel like walking through different parts of a historical Japanese town — from the lively main street, out to the quiet shrine, deeper into the bamboo grove, and finally to the secluded onsen.

---

## 🏮 Village Core

**Main Village Street**
- Central artery of the island — the primary path connecting key buildings
- Lined with stone lanterns, torii-style arches, and seasonal flowers (cherry blossoms, chrysanthemums)
- Market stalls or shop-style furniture clusters can punctuate the street
- Nook's Cranny and the Able Sisters should anchor this area once upgraded

---

## ⛩️ Shrine District

- Dedicated area with a Shinto shrine aesthetic
- Key items: torii gates, stone lanterns, offering boxes, lucky cat furniture, zen cushions
- Surrounded by white and red flowers (likely white pansies + red tulips)
- Cherry blossom trees for seasonal atmosphere
- Raised slightly with terraforming if possible — shrines traditionally sit elevated
- See also: [Items & Furniture → Shrine items](items-and-furniture.md#shrine-items)

---

## 🎋 Bamboo Grove

- A dense stand of bamboo in a quieter part of the island
- Bamboo shoots planted in neat rows or natural-looking clusters
- Paths through the grove use earthy, worn-looking patterns (dirt or wood)
- Can include a small rest area — stone bench, bamboo basket, tea set
- Use the Dense Orchard Method (transplanting adult bamboo) for an immediately full-looking grove — see [Trees & Nature → Bamboo Grove](trees-and-nature.md#bamboo-grove)
- See also: [Items & Furniture → Bamboo items](items-and-furniture.md#bamboo-items)

---

## 🪦 Graveyard

- Small, atmospheric area — peaceful rather than spooky
- Grave markers (headstones), stone lanterns, overgrown look with moss-adjacent flowers
- Dark flowers: black roses, blue hyacinths, or purple pansies
- Possibly adjacent to the shrine district to reinforce the spiritual theme

---

## ♨️ Onsen

- Secluded hot spring area, ideally near water or a cliff edge
- Items: outdoor bath, hot spring stones, bamboo fence, towel racks
- Surrounded by pine trees or cedar trees for privacy
- A small changing area with wooden furniture nearby
- See also: [Items & Furniture → Onsen items](items-and-furniture.md#onsen-items)

---

## 🏛️ Museum Placement

- Museum should be placed where it doesn't disrupt the main Japanese aesthetic
- Options: tuck it near the beach or into a natural cliffside setting
- Surround with stone paths and simple plantings to blend it in
- Avoid placing it on the main village street where it may feel out of place

---

## 🏡 Villager Housing Areas

- Villager houses should be clustered by "neighbourhood" feel rather than scattered randomly
- Aim for 2–3 small clusters with interior paths connecting them
- Each house exterior should be customised to reinforce the Japanese theme where possible
- House plots should be decided before serious terraforming begins to avoid conflicts
- See also: [Villagers](villagers.md) for resident wishlist and thematic notes

---

## 🏔️ Future Terraforming Notes

- Raise the shrine district by at least one cliff level
- Create a natural-looking river path that flows past the bamboo grove
- Use cliff edges to frame the onsen area for privacy
- Avoid excessive symmetry — natural, organic layouts are more true to the theme
- Waterfall placement should feel intentional: near the shrine or bamboo grove
- Hold off on heavy terraforming until 3-star rating is achieved and K.K. Slider has visited
